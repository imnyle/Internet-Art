Utopia or Dystopia?

“Even though the social network sites offer different cultural spaces for self-expression, at the same time, they also dominate cultural imaginations of diasporic queer bodies and impact (sometimes positively and sometimes negatively) their cultural identity formations. . . they can be liberatory cultural spaces; however, since they are not ideology free, they can also . . . be oppressive by perpetuating some of the dominant or oppressive ideologies.”

- Ahment Atay, Globalization’s Impact on
Cultural Identity Formation: Queer DIasporic Males in Cyberspace
