“Identity—queer identity—has long been connected to issues and ideas of Space.
Queers are classified by position:
in or out,
    top or bottom,
       fringe or mainstream.
There are gay districts, dyke bars, drag strips.
In these spaces, issues of community and self are framed.
In these spaces, as we have heard over and over again,


the personal is the political.

But how is that nebulously defined queer identity framed in cyberspace?”
        - Holly Willis and Mikki Halpin, When The Personal Becomes Digital
